Hello, I’m Joseph!

I’m the Assistant Sports Analyst at Silver Bulletin ​, where I help build statistical models across the NBA, NFL, and NCAA and collaborate with Nate Silver on systems like quarterback ratings, Elo models, and team impact frameworks. My work blends modeling, large-scale data engineering, and longform writing aimed at making complex ideas intuitive.

I also serve as a Basketball Analytics Associate for the Mexico City Capitanes in the NBA G-League, developing proprietary metrics, tracking systems, and scouting tools to support player evaluation and team decision-making. I’m especially interested in advantage creation, value estimation, and how teams maximize efficiency under real-world constraints.

In addition to my sports analytics work, I’m a Research Assistant at the Burning Glass Institute ​, using large-scale labor-market and skills data to model how technology, policy, and employer decisions shape economic opportunity.

At Rutgers University–New Brunswick, I double-majored in Computer Science and Business Analytics, with much of my coursework and research spanning statistics, predictive modeling, and information theory. I care a lot about how human behavior interacts with statistical systems.

You can find some of my writing on my blog, The Zone Master ​, or reach me at joseph.a.george@gmail.com​.
